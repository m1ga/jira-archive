Chris Barber 2014-01-10 Please try running the following at the command line: {quote} adb kill-server adb devices ti info -t android {quote} If your device is listed in the "adb devices" output, then it's definitely connected. If it's connected, verify that the device is listed under the "Connected Android Devices" section of the "ti info" output. If it's not listed, then this can be caused by a number of things. Here's the most common that comes to mind: 1. Double check that the "state" of the connected device is "device". If it's not, then your device may be prompting you asking something like "allow usb debugging?" Make sure you say OK on the device. 2. If you have more than one Android SDK installed and you tell Titanium to use one, but you have the other Android SDK in your PATH. This will cause conflicts and mess things up.
