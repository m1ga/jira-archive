  1. Pedro Enrique 2014-07-07 Passing arguments to the send method on a GET request is not allowed. The example in the Kitchen Sink is wrong. The reason this worked in the past is that the ASI library used internally manipulated the request and sometimes changed the GET to a POST, other times it appended the arguments to the url. It is the responsibility of the developer to url-encode the arguments of the request. Documentation: http://docs.appcelerator.com/titanium/latest/#!/api/Titanium.Network.HTTPClient-method-send Kitchen Sink PR: https://github.com/appcelerator/KitchenSink/pull/27
