  1. Kota Iguchi 2018-05-15 Note: Internal implementation: This is because Ti.UI.ScrollView uses subclass of Windows.UI.Xaml.Controls.Control internally that has IsEnabled property which is capable of "graying out" the appearance of the child views. On the other hand, Ti.UI.View uses subclass of Windows.UI.Xaml.Controls.Panel internally and it doesn't have IsEnabled property. This is the reason why ScrollViewer is capable of graying out child views. I'm looking for a workaround, a way to enable graying out even on Panel.
